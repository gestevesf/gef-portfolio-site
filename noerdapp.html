Overview

Summary

The Nœrd Kantine’s application is a mobile app designed to make it easier for customers to purchase items and check the week’s daily menus. It allows customers to charge their account with money, which they can then use to purchase items or pay for their lunch in the canteen.

Challenge

The main challenge was to redesign the app so it could be easier to use, visually appealing, and could handle the transactional nature of the canteen environment.

Solution

I decided to redesign a new version of the canteen application that was more modern and user-friendly. I opted for a tab bar design that would allow users to access any part of the app at any time during their journey.

New Features

Tab Bar

With a new tab bar on the bottom customers can easily access any point of the app with only one tap instead of two, without loosing visibility over the screen.

Charge Account

This feature allows customers to add money to their account using a credit or debit card. The customers can also view their and current balance and transaction history. As it is the most important features of the app, I spent more time looking into a more intuitive way for the users to accomplish this step.

Daily Menus

The app provides customers with the daily menus for the entire week. Which before the app only had a link that redirected the users to the website’s canteen, making them leaving the app.

Profile

Customers can manage their profile information, including their name, email address, and password. Which is now more organized and visually aligned with the rest of the app.

News

Customers can view new information, special days or closing days and new deals by the canteen.

New Looks

Design

I wanted the app to be visually appealing and user-friendly. I kept the same palette that already had a clean and modern design. The tab bar design makes it easy for users to navigate the app, and the large buttons and clear font make it easy for users to understand the functionality.

Final Thoughts

Next Steps

  1. Adding and displaying nutritional and allergen information for each dish in the menu tab will give the users better information to their decisions.
  2. Customers will be able to order food directly from the app. They will be able to select their desired items and pay for them using the funds in their account. The app would also provides an estimated time for the order to be ready for pickup.
  3. Customers will be able to view their order history, including the items they ordered, the price, and the date and time of the order.

Conclusion

The canteen application is an excellent example of how a mobile app can improve the customer experience in a transactional environment, in my opinion. By re-designing the app, it is now easier to use and visually appealing, customers are more likely to use it more often without needing the staff’s help.
